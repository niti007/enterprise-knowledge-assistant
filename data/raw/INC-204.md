# Incident Report — INC-204

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Severity:** P1
**System Affected:** Payment-Service
**Owner Team:** Billing
**Incident Lead:** Priya Sharma
**Referenced SOP:** SOP-17 — Payment Service Restoration

## Summary

On 2026-03-12, a P1 incident was raised against **Payment-Service** due to
Auth-DB hitting connection limits. The outage caused cascading failures in **Auth-DB** which depends
on Payment-Service for critical operations.

## Impact

- Payment-Service became unavailable for approximately 47 minutes.
- Downstream service **Auth-DB** experienced elevated error rates (>30%).
- Customer-facing operations managed by the **Billing** team were degraded.

## Root Cause Analysis

The root cause was identified as: **Auth-DB hitting connection limits**. Investigation conducted by
**Priya Sharma** (Team Lead, Billing) confirmed that Auth-DB has a hard
dependency on Payment-Service; when Payment-Service saturated its connection pool, all downstream
callers including Auth-DB began failing immediately.

## Resolution

Incident was resolved following **SOP-17** (Payment Service Restoration).
Steps taken:
1. Paged on-call engineer via PagerDuty escalation per SOP-05.
2. Identified offending query pattern in Payment-Service logs.
3. Applied connection pool limit increase + circuit breaker configuration.
4. Verified Auth-DB recovery via health-check endpoints.
